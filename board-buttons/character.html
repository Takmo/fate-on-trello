Introduction to Fate

Character Info

Characters are the heart and soul of Fate Core/Accelerated, and your character will be your avatar within the game world. Just like you, your character is a complex individual in an envolving world. Their thoughts, feelings, and backgrounds might reflect your own, or they might be a new adventure entirely.

Aspects are five short phrases that describe the most significant elements about your character. You can see sample aspects below, but you have free reign to pick and choose your character's aspects as desired. Pay special attention to your character's High Concept and Trouble, as they serve special purposes in the game.

In addition to serving as roleplaying guides, aspects can also influence your rolls while confronting obstacles. You begin each game session with 3 Fate Points (unless your game master says otherwise), and when a situation warrants it, you can pay one of your points to add +1 before any roll, as long as the obstacle would logically be easier to overcome because of one of your character's aspects.

Because my character has the aspect Windhelm's Best Locksmith, I would like to pay one Fate Point (and invoke that aspect) to gain +2 when unlocking this door.

Aspects are also useful in replenishing your spent Fate Points. Whereas you can pay a Fate Point to gain a positive advantage based on an aspect, the game master (or even other players) may pay you a Fate Point to compell your character into a negative situation.

Because your character has the aspect Can't Resist a Challenge, she is compelled to pick a fight with the guard bragging at the bar. Good luck getting into the museum now. You gain one Fate Point.

With these uses in mind, give your character a name and tell us what makes them special...

Character Skills

Skills describe what your character is good and not-so-good at. When your character engages in activities related to one of these skills, you can include these bonsues on related rolls.

For new characters, Fate Core recommends the following distribution of skills:

  • 1x Great (+4) skill
  • 2x Good (+3) skills
  • 3x Fair (+2) skills
  • 4x Average (+1) skills

Character Approaches

Approaches are adverbs that describe how your character prefers to solve their problems. There are six approaches in all, and a character can use any approach to solve their problem. However, they naturally excel at certain types of approaches. When a character attempts to complete an action using an approach they are skilled with, you can include that bonus on related roles.

For new characters, Fate Accelerated recommends the following distribution of skills:

  • 1x Good (+3) approach
  • 2x Fair (+2) approaches
  • 2x Average (+1) approaches
  • 1x Mediocre (+0) approach

Looking for something more feature-full? Click here to switch to the Fate Core skill system.

Stunts

Stunts describe special ways your character uses skills/approaches to perform an action. Think of these like a signature move your character uses in a fight, or a special skill they have in certain environments.
